Chapter 0 - Why a VariEze?

The year is 1985, and I see on TV a segment about Burt Rutam, an aerospace engineer who had designed radical airplanes, including the VariEze, VariViggen, and Voyager. The image of the three airplanes flying in formation has been hard-coded in my brain. Then, as I worked through my aerospace engineering degree, I got some exposure to composites and begain thinking aobut builind a Rutan design. I was dead centered ton building a Long-EZ and begain getting involved wiht Rutan Aircraft Flying Experience, where I was able to get up close to Canards for the very first time. A VariEze project became available, and I started taking a closer look at the aircraft. It has decent cruise and range for my mission, and it will be the perfect platform to learn to build composite aircraft. So here we are, about to embark on a great adventure building an airplane at home. Some folks have taken decades to build their airplanes. The plans do say it should take 340 man-hours for Part I (manufacturing of basic airframe). We shall see where I land.

So why a VariEze?

  • Simple to build and re-build
  • Plans-built, so no dependency on factory kits
  • Revolutionary design that disrupted the industry
  • Beautiful aircraft
  • Fits my mission: 1200-mile trip for two individuals for about 8 hours with a break in between
